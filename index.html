Behind the Statistics:
A Deep Dive into Malaysian Crime

Overview of Crime Distribution Across Malaysia

In 2017, Malaysia recorded a total of 99,168 crimes, which included various categories such as property crimes, violent crimes, and drug-related offenses. Kuala Lumpur and Selangor were undoubtedly the most dangerous areas, reaching a peak of 13,482 cases reported in 2017 alone. Among these crimes, theft emerged as the most prevalent, accounting for nearly 32% of all reported offenses.

Trends in Crime Rates

The area chart shows a notable increase in both violent and property crimes over time, with motorcycle theft emerging as a dominant issue. Understanding these trends can help inform effective prevention strategies.

Gender Disparities in Crime

The data reveals significant gender disparities in crime involvement, with men being disproportionately represented. This raises questions about social norms and the structure of the criminal justice system.

Youth & Crime

The disparities in crime rates between young males and females point to underlying societal challenges and pressures. Addressing these trends is crucial for reducing future criminal behavior.
