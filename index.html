Behind the Statistics:
A Deep Dive into Malaysian Crime

Where Are the Crime Hotspots in Malaysia?

In 2017, Malaysia recorded a total of 99,168 crimes, which included various categories such as property crimes, violent crimes, and drug-related offenses. Kuala Lumpur and Selangor were undoubtedly the most dangerous areas, reaching a peak of 13,482 cases reported in 2017 alone. Among these crimes, theft emerged as the most prevalent, accounting for nearly 32% of all reported offenses.

How Dangerous is Southeast Asia?

Malaysia's crime index stands out as the highest in Southeast Asia at an outstanding 58.55.

Crime Index is an estimation of the overall level of crime in a given city or country. Levels below 20 are considered very low, between 20 and 40 as low, 40 and 60 as moderate, 60 and 80 as high, and levels above 80 as very high.
